Habits are automatic, non-effortful, and often hard to break. Habits can keep us on track when motivation lags.

Q: So how do you get your exercise to be more habitual?

A: Regular practice, helped along by lots of reminders!

  1. Establish a regular time of day when you will be active.
  2. Keep your workout clothes and gym bag serve as reminders - put them in plain view.
    1. If you can, get a nice set of workout clothes that makes you feel good and excited about your activity.
    2. Wear these clothes only for your activity - let the clothes signal action.
  3. Place a note on the door (Today I'm boosting my mood with activity!) or inside your athletic shoe (Wear me today for a better mood).
  4. Notice yourself feeling depressed or low???
    1. Let feelings of stress or low mood signal the perfect time to exercise. Activity is a terrific response to stress.
    2. Rate your mood just before and just after you are active - these ratings and MoveME will remind you how well it worked for next time.
