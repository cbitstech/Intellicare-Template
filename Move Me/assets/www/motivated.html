People commonly wait to feel motivated BEFORE they actually do something, whether that’s physical activity or something else (grocery shopping, calling your mother). Motivation doesn't actually work that way - you must DO and MAKE SOME PROGRESS before internal motivation kicks in.

It's better to start with something outside of yourself, initially, for the energy to be physically active. For example, you could

  • Choose a start date for your new routine (think: New Year's!)
  • Take a picture of that new swimsuit you want to fit into in June.
  • Pay yourself each time you workout - $1 per day can really add up fast!

Next, use prompts to remind yourself of these external motivators, such as:

  1. Set calendar reminders (you can use MOVEME or your own calendar).
  2. Leave your workout clothes or sneakers out as a reminder.
  3. Tell a friend about your new routine - they might want to join!
  4. Plan to listen to your favorite audiobook or podcast while working out, or restrict your TV watching only to when you’re on a machine at the gym.
  5. Create playlists to listen to while you walk, jog, or even as you get dressed for activity.
  6. Imagine yourself at the end of your physical activity - SMILE!!!

And REMEMBER: a bad mood is a REASON TO exercise, not a reason to skip it!

Use these external motivators to get on your routine - MOVEME will teach you how to recognize the internal motivators as you move forward!
