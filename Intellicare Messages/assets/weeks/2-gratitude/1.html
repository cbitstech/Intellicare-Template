If you are like most people, you think too much about what goes wrong and not enough about what goes right in your life. Of course, sometimes it makes sense for us to analyze upsetting events so that we can learn from them and avoid them in the future. However, people tend to spend more time thinking about the negatives in life than is helpful. Worse, this tendency to focus on the negative sets us up for feeling anxious, sad, or irritable, and deprives us of moments of joy, contentment, and happiness.

One way to keep this from happening is to develop our ability to think about the good parts of life. Most of us don't spend nearly as much time analyzing good events as we do analyzing the events that upset us! That is why this is a skill that needs practice. As you become better at focusing on the good in your life, you will likely become more grateful for what you have and more hopeful about the future. So let's get started.
