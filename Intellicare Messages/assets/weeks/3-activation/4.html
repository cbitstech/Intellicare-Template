Sometimes it's Hard to do Positive Things

It is important to learn to do positive things even when you don't feel like it. Try acting like you would if you were feeling good, rather than acting how you actually feel. Waiting until you feel like doing a positive activity won't help you have control over how you feel. Doing positive activities even when you don't feel like it is what will improve your mood. Action before motivation!

Pleasurable activities are those that bring a sense of happiness or contentment simply from doing them. Spending time with friends or working on a favorite hobby are examples of pleasurable activities.

Accomplishment activities are those that bring a sense of achievement or control over your life. Accomplishment is that feeling you get when you finish something, whether it's something big or something small. Accomplishment can come from something as simple as making a really great cup of coffee or something bigger, like finishing some home repairs. Crossing things off your to-do list, like finishing chores, doing the laundry, or paying bills are often great ways to get a sense of accomplishment.
