This week is about helping you rethink negative thought patterns. The way we think affects how we feel. For example, if a friend gets angry with you, and you think, "Oh no, I'm going to lose this friend," you'd likely feel sad or anxious. If your thought was, "This person always gets angry, and never understands me," you might feel angry. But what if, instead, you thought, "This person has a lot going on and is probably stressed"? You'd probably not feel bad at all, and might even feel sympathetic.

Everyone has negative thoughts during stressful or challenging situations. These thoughts are often "automatic," which means that they just pop into our heads. Sometimes we are barely aware of them!
