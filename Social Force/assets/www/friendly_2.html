Speaking with confidence means that you can communicate your wants and needs in a productive manner. This takes both verbal and nonverbal skill.

Body language communicates our feelings and attitudes, and projects how willing we are to listen to others, all before we say the first word. Body language that helps us communicate with others includes SOFTEN:

  • Smiling
  • Opening your arms
  • Leaning Forward
  • Touching the other person on the arm or hand
  • Eye contact
  • Nodding

You can do any or all of these nonverbal gestures to communicate openness and confidence to another person.

All together, your body language, tone of voice, and the words you use create a picture of you as communicator. Using these gestures, a light and easy tone of voice, and actively listening to someone will make you seem confident even if/when you don't feel it!
